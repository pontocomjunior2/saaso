## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** B2B SaaS CRM for Brazilian SMBs — inside-sales / SDR automation on WhatsApp (the dominant first-touch channel in Brazil, ~99% smartphone penetration and >96% of businesses using it for customer contact).

**User Population:**
- **End-users of the AI output:** inbound leads messaging SMB sales pipelines on WhatsApp (PT-BR, mobile, informal register, fragmented multi-bubble style).
- **Operators configuring the AI:** SMB founders, sales managers, and SDR team leads authoring the agent prompt / knowledge base inside Saaso.
- **Downstream consumers of the AI decision:** human SDRs who confirm the hybrid-qualified advance (D-01) and act on handoff signals.

**Stakes Level:** High — not life-safety, but directly commercial. A single bad reply reaches a real prospect on the company's primary revenue channel, is archived in the lead's WhatsApp history, and is attributable to the brand. False qualification pollutes pipeline analytics and wastes SDR capacity; missed qualification or late handoff loses deals. WhatsApp policy violations (spam thresholds, undisclosed AI) can trigger number-level bans that kill the company's inbound channel entirely.

**Output Consequence:** Each structured reply drives four concurrent real-world effects: (1) a message is sent to a lead on WhatsApp, (2) a `CardActivity` is persisted for audit, (3) a Kanban badge + notification may fire for the SDR, and (4) the pipeline's qualification funnel metrics are incremented. All four are acted on by humans or downstream automations — there is no silent-failure mode.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

The dimensions below are written in the language an experienced SDR or sales-ops lead would use when reviewing a transcript, not in ML-eval jargon. Each is a rubric ingredient for Section 5.

---

**Dimension: Qualification signal correctness (hybrid-mode precision)**
- Good: `mark_qualified: true` is set only when the conversation surfaces explicit, citable evidence of the qualification criteria encoded in the agent prompt (e.g., budget stated, decision-maker identified, pain articulated, time-frame given) — and `qualification_reason` quotes or paraphrases the exact user turn that justified it.
- Bad: Agent flags `mark_qualified: true` after a generic "tenho interesse" / "quero saber mais", or invents a justification not present in the conversation, or omits `qualification_reason`.
- Stakes: Critical. Pollutes the SDR's work queue and the pipeline conversion metrics; degrades the SDR's trust in the agent.
- Source: Inside-sales practitioner rubrics — BANT/MEDDIC scorecards require a dimension-by-dimension evidentiary basis before a lead is marked qualified; "Need must score ≥3/5" is the common threshold (prospeo.io 2026 BANT rubric).

**Dimension: Handoff timing and escalation cues**
- Good: `request_handoff: true` is raised on clear escalation signals — pricing negotiation, legal/contract/tax questions, explicit "quero falar com uma pessoa / atendente / vendedor", anger/frustration, repeated failure of the agent to answer the same question, or any topic outside the configured KB/scope.
- Bad: Agent keeps responding through a pricing negotiation; agent answers a legal or fiscal question from its own inference; agent ignores a direct human request; agent escalates too early on routine questions it should handle (false-handoff wastes SDR capacity).
- Stakes: Critical. Under-escalation creates brand/legal exposure (unauthorised commitments, wrong fiscal info, frustrated prospects). Over-escalation erodes the product value-prop (SDR would rather handle every message themselves).
- Source: Production chatbot literature on "smart escalation logic" — trigger on turn-count ≥4, negative sentiment, explicit human request, or >2 failed attempt cycles (Galileo 2026 agent failure-modes guide). Matches Brazilian WhatsApp norms where a direct "falar com humano" is treated by prospects as a hard contract.

**Dimension: Tone, register, and brand-safety in PT-BR**
- Good: Matches the tone encoded in the agent persona (typically informal-professional Brazilian Portuguese, short messages, emojis only if the persona allows), avoids over-promising, never fabricates pricing / SLA / feature availability, discloses AI presence when the operator's prompt or policy requires it.
- Bad: Robotic-template phrasing ("Prezado(a) cliente, conforme solicitado..."), English fragments, made-up product features, made-up discounts or prices, impersonating a specific human SDR, denying it is an AI when asked.
- Stakes: High. Brand damage compounds across the WhatsApp archive; one screenshotted bad reply circulates. Meta's 2026 WhatsApp AI policy also pushes toward AI-use disclosure in customer-facing flows.
- Source: WhatsApp Business 2026 policy (customer-facing disclosures of AI use), general CX/chatbot failure literature (hallucinated policies, invented order numbers, confident fabrication — Monte Carlo, Sparkout 2026).

**Dimension: Multi-turn fragmentation handling (`should_respond` discipline)**
- Good: When the lead is mid-thought ("oi" ... "tudo bem?" ... "queria saber preço"), agent returns `should_respond: false` on the first fragments and produces a single consolidated reply once intent is clear — mirroring how a human SDR waits for the prospect to finish typing.
- Bad: Agent replies to each bubble separately, producing 3 back-to-back AI messages to 3 incoming bubbles (looks robotic, triggers WhatsApp spam heuristics, annoys the lead). Or the opposite: agent holds indefinitely and never replies.
- Stakes: High. Fragmentation is the dominant WhatsApp interaction pattern in Brazil; getting it wrong is the most visible "this is a bot" signal to a Brazilian prospect. Also risks rate-limit / spam flags on the WhatsApp number.
- Source: Brazilian WhatsApp conversational norms (native bubble-fragmented messaging); explicit product decision in CONTEXT.md D-04 (prompt-driven, no backend debounce).

**Dimension: Structured-output schema fidelity**
- Good: Every decision turn returns a JSON object parseable by `StructuredReplySchema`, with all 7 fields present, `reply` non-null when `should_respond: true`, `qualification_reason` non-null when `mark_qualified: true`, `handoff_reason` non-null when `request_handoff: true`, `suggested_next_stage_id` a real stage of the card's pipeline.
- Bad: Parse failures (forcing D-18 fallback), `suggested_next_stage_id` pointing at a stage of a different pipeline or a deleted stage, `mark_qualified: true` with `qualification_reason: null`, long prose outside the JSON envelope.
- Stakes: High. Parse-fallbacks silently degrade the agent from "decisioning agent" to "text generator"; bad stage IDs break the 1-click advance CTA (D-25) and train SDRs to distrust the feature.
- Source: Code-based eval dimension from ai-evals.md ("Output structure validity"); cross-referenced with the Section 4 pitfalls (strict mode is not 100%, `$ref`, refusal handling).

**Dimension: Response latency and throughput (operator-perceived)**
- Good: p50 ≤ 1.5s, p95 ≤ 4s from inbound webhook to outbound send; held turns (`should_respond:false`) resolve into a consolidated reply within a natural wait (typically <10s of no further bubbles).
- Bad: >10s delay makes the lead think they are being ignored on WhatsApp; >30s causes double-messaging from the lead ("alô?"), which then cascades into fragmentation confusion.
- Stakes: Medium. Degrades UX and fragmentation-handling quality, but not catastrophic.
- Source: Brazilian WhatsApp response-time expectations (informal-synchronous channel — leads expect "fast" not "instant"), cross-referenced with Section 4 latency budget.

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

Domain-specific manifestations — not generic LLM failures. Each maps to at least one rubric dimension above and informs Section 1 Critical Failure Modes.

1. **Premature qualification on weak intent.** Lead says "tenho interesse" or "me conta mais" and agent sets `mark_qualified: true`, fabricating a `qualification_reason`. SDR confirms because the badge is green, pipeline conversion metrics inflate artificially, and the lead drops off two turns later. Root cause: prompt did not pin "qualified" to a concrete evidentiary threshold; model default-biases toward assertive output in structured mode.
2. **Silent scope creep → hallucinated policy/price/SLA.** Lead asks "qual o prazo de implantação?" or "vocês emitem NF?"; agent answers confidently with made-up numbers because the KB is silent. Produces a brand-damaging transcript and sets up a broken-promise problem downstream. Mitigation pattern: handoff-on-unknown, not invent-on-unknown.
3. **Missed handoff on explicit human request.** Lead types "prefiro falar com alguém" / "passa pro vendedor, pfv" and the model continues its qualification script because the keyword-based `shouldRequireHandoff` is removed (D-10) and the prompt did not enumerate escalation phrases. In Brazilian sales culture this is a hard contract — continuing to bot-reply kills the deal.
4. **Fragmentation cascade: 3 replies to 3 bubbles.** Operator forgets the "IMPORTANTE" banner instructions, the prompt has no `should_respond` guidance, and the agent replies per-bubble. Triggers WhatsApp spam heuristics and makes the bot obvious.
5. **Cross-pipeline / stale `suggested_next_stage_id`.** Agent picks a stage ID from its training context that does not belong to the card's current pipeline (pipelines were edited after the conversation started), causing the 1-click CTA to misfire. Covered by D-25 fallback but must be measured.
6. **Refusal leaking as empty reply.** OpenAI safety refusal arrives as `output[].content[].type === 'refusal'` (Pitfall #4) and, if mis-handled, the lead gets an empty message or a generic parse-fallback text that makes no contextual sense. Must be surfaced as `AGENT_HELD reason=model_refusal`.
7. **Memory drift after summarization.** Summarizer overwrites `AgentConversation.summary` and drops a critical fact the operator expected to persist (e.g., the lead's stated budget). Current turn re-asks the same question, lead gets annoyed.
8. **AI-disclosure gap vs. WhatsApp 2026 policy.** Lead asks "isso é um robô?"; agent denies or evades. This is both a trust failure and a policy-alignment risk under Meta's January 2026 AI-on-WhatsApp rules which push "customer-facing disclosures should reflect any AI use". Prompt must carry an honest-disclosure clause.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

- **LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados, Lei 13.709/2018, ANPD-enforced).** Conversational content on WhatsApp is personal data. Saaso as data controller (and/or processor, depending on contract with the SMB tenant) must: (a) have a lawful basis — typically *legítimo interesse* for inbound commercial triage, *consentimento* for outbound marketing; (b) provide a data-subject rights channel (access, correction, deletion, portability); (c) not retain conversations beyond necessity; (d) log who/what processed each turn — the existing `CardActivity` + `AgentMessage.metadata` trail directly satisfies this. **Implication for this phase:** the Section 6 guardrails must include an AI-disclosure / opt-out pattern, and data retention of `AgentMessage.metadata` should be covered by the tenant's privacy policy.
- **WhatsApp Business Solution Terms + Meta January 2026 AI Policy.** General-purpose AI assistants on the WhatsApp Business Platform are prohibited as of 2026-01-15. Purpose-specific business bots (support, bookings, **sales qualification**) remain explicitly allowed — Saaso's Phase 5 agent is on the allowed side because it is scoped to qualification/triage for a defined pipeline. Requirements: user-initiated session (24h window) or approved template messages; honour opt-outs; avoid spam thresholds. **Implication for this phase:** do not attempt open-ended generalist behaviour in the prompt; preserve the qualification scope; log opt-outs as first-class activities.
- **Brazilian CDC (Código de Defesa do Consumidor).** An AI reply that promises a price, delivery date, or SLA can create a binding consumer expectation. Guardrails must prevent the agent from asserting commercial terms that are not in the KB.
- **No sector-specific regimes** (healthcare/finance/legal) apply by default at the product level; tenant-specific compliance (e.g., a legal or medical SMB using Saaso) is out of scope this phase but should be noted as "tenant responsibility" in the agent editor help copy.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Zod 3.x + direct OpenAI Responses API (no orchestration framework)

**Version:** Zod (existing project dep, 3.x) + OpenAI Responses API (`text.format: { type: 'json_schema', strict: true }`)

**Rationale:**
CONTEXT.md locks a NestJS handler-pipeline architecture (D-08) with Zod-validated structured output and explicit no-function-calling scope (D-03). Every required capability — multi-turn memory (Prisma `AgentMessage` + `AgentConversation.summary`), checkpoint-like resume (`AgentConversationStatus`), retry (BullMQ `agent-retry`), audit (`CardActivity`) — is already owned by existing modules. Adding LangChain.js / LangGraph / OpenAI Agents SDK / Vercel AI SDK would re-abstract the pipeline the team deliberately designed as 5 isolated `@Injectable()` handlers. The move: extend the existing `AiService` with `generateStructuredResponse<T>(prompt, userMessage, zodSchema, options)` that uses OpenAI Responses API's native `text.format: { type: 'json_schema', strict: true }` and validates with Zod — ~40 LOC, zero new runtime deps, full control, preserves auditor plug-in points.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| Vercel AI SDK (`ai` package, `generateObject`) | Provider-swappable Zod+JSON wrapper is appealing, but costs one new dep and replaces the hand-rolled OpenAI Responses API wiring already in `AiService`. Only acceptable if the team later wants provider portability. |
| LangChain.js / LangGraph | Fights D-08 (manual handler pipeline) and D-03 (no tools). Anti-pattern #1 from ai-frameworks.md: "Using LangChain for simple chatbots — direct SDK call is less code, faster, and easier to debug." |
| OpenAI Agents SDK | Designed around tool-using autonomous agents; Phase 5 explicitly excludes function calling. Would add indirection for no capability gain. |
| CrewAI | Multi-agent orchestration — not applicable (single conversational agent this phase). |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial — already tied to OpenAI Responses API via existing `AiService`. Zod schema is provider-agnostic; swapping providers later means rewriting only the `AiService.generateStructuredResponse` internals, not the handlers or the `StructuredReplySchema`.

### Entry Point Pattern

Minimal working example for the conversational structured-output pattern against the OpenAI Responses API. This is the shape `AiService.generateStructuredResponse` implements internally, then the handler consumes it:

```typescript
// 1) Define schema ONCE (apps/api/src/agent/schemas/structured-reply.schema.ts)
import { z } from 'zod';

export const StructuredReplySchema = z.object({
  should_respond: z.boolean(),
  reply: z.string().nullable(),
  mark_qualified: z.boolean(),
  qualification_reason: z.string().nullable(),
  suggested_next_stage_id: z.string().nullable(),
  request_handoff: z.boolean(),
  handoff_reason: z.string().nullable(),
});
export type StructuredReply = z.infer<typeof StructuredReplySchema>;

// 2) Call pattern inside AiService (simplified — see Section 4 for full code)
import { zodToJsonSchema } from 'zod-to-json-schema';

const jsonSchema = zodToJsonSchema(StructuredReplySchema, {
  target: 'openApi3',
  $refStrategy: 'none', // inline everything; strict mode rejects $ref chains
});

const body = {
  model: 'gpt-4o-mini',
  instructions: compiledPrompt,          // system/persona/KB/etc.
  input: conversationInputArray,          // multi-turn (see Section 4)
  max_output_tokens: 800,
  temperature: 0.3,
  text: {
    format: {
      type: 'json_schema',
      name: 'structured_reply',           // required in Responses API
      strict: true,                       // hard-enforced schema
      schema: jsonSchema,                 // inlined Zod->JSON Schema
    },
  },
  store: false,
};

const res = await fetch(`${apiBaseUrl}/responses`, {
  method: 'POST',
  headers: { Authorization: `Bearer ${apiKey}`, 'Content-Type': 'application/json' },
  body: JSON.stringify(body),
});
const data = await res.json();

// Refusal and parse detection — explained in Section 4 pitfalls
const raw = data.output_text ?? extractAssistantText(data);
const parsed = StructuredReplySchema.safeParse(JSON.parse(raw));
if (!parsed.success) {
  // Parse-failure fallback per D-18: treat raw as reply, log AGENT_PARSE_FALLBACK
}
```

### Key Abstractions

| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `text.format.type: 'json_schema'` | Responses API field (replaces legacy Chat Completions `response_format`) that attaches a schema contract to the model output. | Every call that must return parseable JSON. Phase 5 uses it on every agent reply call. |
| `strict: true` | Flag under `text.format` that enables token-level constrained decoding. Schema **must** declare every property in `required` and set `additionalProperties: false`. | Always on for `StructuredReplySchema`. Off if a schema can't meet the constraints (not the case here). |
| `input` array with role items | Multi-turn conversation history passed as `[{ role: 'user'\|'assistant', content: string }, ...]` alongside `instructions`. | Window-memory loader (`ConversationHistoryLoader`) builds this from the last N `AgentMessage` rows. |
| `output_text` / `output[]` | Convenience flat string vs. full message array with refusal detection. For `json_schema` outputs, `output_text` is the JSON string. | Read `output_text` first; fall back to scanning `output[]` for `type: 'message' / content[].type: 'output_text'`. |
| Zod `.safeParse` | Second line of defense after strict mode (it has bugs; see Pitfall #3). Narrows to `StructuredReply` or returns error. | Every structured call before the handler cascades. |
| `AiService.generateStructuredResponse<T>` | New generic method we add; internalises all provider specifics so handlers stay pure. | Called once by `StructuredReplyGenerator`. |

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Legacy `response_format` won't work on the Responses API.** The Chat Completions field is `response_format: { type: 'json_schema', json_schema: {...} }`. The Responses API uses `text.format: { type: 'json_schema', name, strict, schema }`. Existing `AiService.generateResponse` already uses `text.format: { type: 'text' }` — stay consistent or you will get a 400.
2. **Strict mode rejects "optional" fields.** In strict mode every property listed in `properties` MUST appear in `required`, and `additionalProperties` MUST be `false`. Zod `.optional()` converts to an omitted-required property in standard zod-to-json-schema → 400 from OpenAI. Use `.nullable()` instead (the schema in D-17 already does). Prefer `openai-zod-to-json-schema` with `openaiStrictMode: true` if mixing is unavoidable — it rewrites optional → required+nullable automatically.
3. **`strict: true` is not a 100% guarantee.** Multiple community reports in 2025–2026 confirm edge cases (deeply nested unions, long enums) where the model still emits invalid JSON. Always `JSON.parse` inside `try/catch` and `StructuredReplySchema.safeParse` after — fallback per D-18 must never be skipped.
4. **Refusals arrive on a different field.** Refused outputs appear as `output[].content[].type === 'refusal'` (Responses API) with no JSON payload. Code must detect that case explicitly — `JSON.parse` will throw on the refusal message and you'll mis-log it as a parse failure. Treat refusal as `should_respond: false` + `AGENT_HELD` with reason `model_refusal`.
5. **`$ref` kills strict mode in practice.** Zod discriminated unions or reused sub-schemas emit `$ref` by default. Pass `$refStrategy: 'none'` to `zodToJsonSchema` so every sub-schema is inlined. OpenAI does accept some `$defs`, but keeping it inlined avoids the inconsistent error surface.
6. **`max_output_tokens` must fit the largest valid JSON.** `StructuredReplySchema` with a long `reply` + long `qualification_reason` can easily exceed 600 tokens. Budget **≥ 800** for this schema and measure. If the model truncates mid-JSON, strict mode still emits a 200 with invalid JSON — fallback covers it but costs a retry.
7. **Temperature > 0.7 hurts structured outputs noticeably.** Keep `temperature: 0.3` for the decisioning call; use higher temperature only on reply-text generation calls (not applicable here — single-call design).
8. **History ordering matters — OLDEST first.** Responses API reads `input` top-down. Building it newest-first (intuitive from a `orderBy: createdAt desc` query) will confuse the model and inject recency bias in the wrong direction. Reverse after query.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**

| Parameter | Value | Reason |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| `model` | `gpt-4o-mini` (default; overridable via `Agent.profile.model`) | Cheapest tier with full Structured Outputs + Responses API support. Latency ~1–2s for ~800 tokens. Already the existing `AiService` default. |
| `temperature` | `0.3` | Keeps decisioning deterministic; reply text still reads natural. Strict-mode emission degrades above ~0.7. |
| `max_output_tokens` | `800` (default) / `Agent.profile.maxTokens` if present | Fits all 7 fields of `StructuredReplySchema` with long reply + long reasons comfortably. |
| `store` | `false` | We persist history ourselves in `AgentMessage`; no need for OpenAI-side retention. |
| `text.format` | `{ type: 'json_schema', name: 'structured_reply', strict: true, schema }` | Token-level schema enforcement. |

**Core Pattern — `AiService.generateStructuredResponse<T>`**

This is the one new method. Drop-in sibling to existing `generateResponse`. Zero behavioral change to anything else:

```typescript
// apps/api/src/common/services/ai.service.ts (addition)
import { z, ZodTypeAny } from 'zod';
import { zodToJsonSchema } from 'zod-to-json-schema';

export interface StructuredCallOptions extends GenerateAgentResponseOptions {
  /** Prior turns, OLDEST first. Excludes the current user message. */
  history?: Array<{ role: 'user' | 'assistant'; content: string }>;
  /** Optional summary block injected as a system-level reminder. */
  summary?: string | null;
  /** Name for the JSON Schema (must match /^[a-zA-Z0-9_-]+$/). */
  schemaName?: string;
}

export type StructuredResult<T> =
  | { ok: true; data: T; raw: string }
  | { ok: false; reason: 'refusal' | 'parse' | 'provider' | 'empty'; raw: string | null; error?: unknown };

public async generateStructuredResponse<T>(
  systemPrompt: string,
  userMessage: string,
  schema: z.ZodType<T>,
  options: StructuredCallOptions = {},
): Promise<StructuredResult<T>> {
  if (!this.apiKey) {
    return { ok: false, reason: 'provider', raw: null };
  }

  // 1) Build the multi-turn input array. OLDEST first (pitfall #8).
  const input: Array<{ role: 'user' | 'assistant' | 'system'; content: string }> = [];
  if (options.summary) {
    input.push({
      role: 'system',
      content: `Resumo das interações anteriores (use como contexto, não repita literalmente):\n${options.summary}`,
    });
  }
  for (const turn of options.history ?? []) {
    input.push({ role: turn.role, content: turn.content });
  }
  input.push({ role: 'user', content: userMessage.trim() });

  // 2) Inline Zod -> JSON Schema. $refStrategy:'none' avoids strict-mode $ref issues (pitfall #5).
  const jsonSchema = zodToJsonSchema(schema, {
    target: 'openApi3',
    $refStrategy: 'none',
  }) as Record<string, unknown>;

  const body = {
    model: this.normalizeModel(options.model),
    instructions: systemPrompt.trim(),
    input,
    max_output_tokens: this.normalizeMaxTokens(options.maxTokens ?? 800),
    temperature: this.normalizeTemperature(options.temperature ?? 0.3),
    text: {
      format: {
        type: 'json_schema',
        name: options.schemaName ?? 'structured_reply',
        strict: true,
        schema: jsonSchema,
      },
    },
    store: false,
  };

  let response: Response;
  try {
    response = await fetch(`${this.apiBaseUrl}/responses`, {
      method: 'POST',
      headers: { Authorization: `Bearer ${this.apiKey}`, 'Content-Type': 'application/json' },
      body: JSON.stringify(body),
    });
  } catch (error) {
    this.logger.error('Responses API network failure', error instanceof Error ? error.stack : undefined);
    return { ok: false, reason: 'provider', raw: null, error };
  }

  if (!response.ok) {
    const payload = await this.safeParseJson(response);
    this.logger.error(`Responses API ${response.status}: ${this.stringifyPayload(payload)}`);
    return { ok: false, reason: 'provider', raw: null, error: payload };
  }

  const data = (await response.json()) as OpenAiResponsesApiPayload & {
    output?: Array<{ type?: string; content?: Array<{ type?: string; refusal?: string; text?: string }> }>;
  };

  // 3) Refusal detection (pitfall #4).
  const refusal = data.output
    ?.flatMap((i) => i.content ?? [])
    .find((c) => c?.type === 'refusal');
  if (refusal?.refusal) {
    this.logger.warn(`Responses API refusal: ${refusal.refusal}`);
    return { ok: false, reason: 'refusal', raw: refusal.refusal };
  }

  const rawText = this.extractOutputText(data);
  if (!rawText) {
    return { ok: false, reason: 'empty', raw: null };
  }

  // 4) Parse + Zod validation (pitfall #3 — strict is not a 100% guarantee).
  try {
    const jsonValue = JSON.parse(rawText);
    const parsed = schema.safeParse(jsonValue);
    if (!parsed.success) {
      return { ok: false, reason: 'parse', raw: rawText, error: parsed.error };
    }
    return { ok: true, data: parsed.data, raw: rawText };
  } catch (error) {
    return { ok: false, reason: 'parse', raw: rawText, error };
  }
}
```

**Tool Use:** None. Phase 5 is explicitly no-function-calling (D-03). The `StructuredReplySchema` *is* the contract — `request_handoff` and `mark_qualified` replace what tools would otherwise do, and the runner handler cascade acts on those booleans.

**State Management:**

- **Persistence layer (Prisma):**
  - `AgentMessage` — every USER and AGENT turn (AGENT-role only when a real outbound was sent; held turns get a `CardActivity` instead per D-11).
  - `AgentMessage.metadata` (new column, D-12) — stores the full validated `StructuredReply` JSON for each AGENT turn. Source of truth for future auditor.
  - `AgentConversation.summary` — populated asynchronously by `ConversationSummarizer` BullMQ worker (queue `agent-summarize`, concurrency 1 per `conversationId`).
  - `AgentConversation.status` — state machine substitute (`OPEN` / `HANDOFF_REQUIRED` / `CLOSED`). `updatedAt` serves as optimistic-lock token (D-22).
- **Retrieval path (`ConversationHistoryLoader`):**
  ```typescript
  const windowSize = profile?.historyWindow ?? 20;
  const rows = await prisma.agentMessage.findMany({
    where: { conversationId },
    orderBy: { createdAt: 'desc' },
    take: windowSize,
    select: { role: true, content: true, createdAt: true },
  });
  const history = rows
    .reverse()                                       // Pitfall #8: oldest first
    .map((m) => ({
      role: m.role === 'AGENT' ? ('assistant' as const) : ('user' as const),
      content: m.content,
    }));
  ```
- **Handler cascade order (runner, D-11):**
  1. `HandoffHandler.check(reply)` — if `request_handoff` hard-stop, update conversation status, activity, return.
  2. `QualificationHandler.check(reply, card)` — non-terminal; writes `AGENT_QUALIFIED` activity + notification; continues.
  3. Decide outbound: if `should_respond === false` → persist `AGENT_HELD` activity with raw output, skip AGENT `AgentMessage` row.
  4. `OutboundDispatcher.send(reply.reply)` → persist `AgentMessage` AGENT-role row with `metadata = structured reply raw JSON`.

**Context Window Strategy:**

- **Window + summary (D-02).** Default window = 20 turns, default summary threshold = 10 turns (`Agent.profile.historyWindow`, `Agent.profile.summaryThreshold`).
- **Token budget (gpt-4o-mini, 128k context):**
  - System `instructions` (compiled prompt + KB): cap at ~4k tokens via `buildAgentCompiledPrompt` truncation (already truncates KB content to 2200 chars).
  - `summary` block: cap at ~500 tokens (summarizer worker enforces).
  - `history` window (20 × avg 80 tokens): ~1.6k tokens.
  - Current user msg + schema overhead: ~300 tokens.
  - Total input ≈ 6.5k; output ≤ 800. Comfortable headroom.
- **Summarizer worker (`agent-summarize` BullMQ queue):**
  - Trigger: after every AGENT-role `AgentMessage` insert, if `(messageCount % summaryThreshold === 0)`.
  - Runs `AiService.generateResponse` (plain text, not structured) with a "condense these turns into ≤ 500 tokens" prompt.
  - Writes `AgentConversation.summary` (overwrites). On failure, previous summary stays (D-21).
  - Concurrency 1 per `conversationId` (prevents racing writes).

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice. Python "Pydantic" sections are substituted with TypeScript + Zod to match this project.

### Structured Outputs with Zod

The reply schema is the full decisioning contract. One file, exported type used by runner + handlers + tests + future auditor phase.

```typescript
// apps/api/src/agent/schemas/structured-reply.schema.ts
import { z } from 'zod';

export const StructuredReplySchema = z
  .object({
    should_respond: z
      .boolean()
      .describe('true se o agente deve enviar a reply ao lead agora; false para segurar (fragmentação).'),
    reply: z
      .string()
      .nullable()
      .describe('Texto pronto para envio ao lead. null quando should_respond=false.'),
    mark_qualified: z
      .boolean()
      .describe('true se o lead atende aos critérios de qualificação desta etapa.'),
    qualification_reason: z
      .string()
      .nullable()
      .describe('Justificativa curta (1-2 frases) quando mark_qualified=true; null caso contrário.'),
    suggested_next_stage_id: z
      .string()
      .nullable()
      .describe('ID exato de uma etapa válida deste pipeline, ou null.'),
    request_handoff: z
      .boolean()
      .describe('true se o lead precisa de atendimento humano imediato.'),
    handoff_reason: z
      .string()
      .nullable()
      .describe('Justificativa do handoff; null quando request_handoff=false.'),
  })
  .strict();

export type StructuredReply = z.infer<typeof StructuredReplySchema>;
```

**Why every field is `.nullable()` instead of `.optional()`:** OpenAI `strict: true` requires every property to appear in `required[]` and `additionalProperties: false`. `.optional()` would drop the field from `required` and trigger a 400. See Pitfall #2.

**Framework integration.** `AiService.generateStructuredResponse<T>` converts with `zodToJsonSchema(schema, { target: 'openApi3', $refStrategy: 'none' })` and wraps the call in `text.format: { type: 'json_schema', name, strict: true, schema }`. See Section 4 code.

**Retry / validation-failure policy:**

| Failure kind | Action | Activity | Max attempts |
|--------------|--------|----------|--------------|
| `refusal` | Treat as `should_respond: false`; do NOT retry (would likely refuse again). | `AGENT_HELD` with `reason: model_refusal` | 1 |
| `parse` (JSON broken or Zod schema mismatch) | D-18 fallback: raw text → `reply`, `should_respond: true`. Log raw. | `AGENT_PARSE_FALLBACK` with `metadata.raw_output` | 1 (no retry loop — fallback *is* the retry) |
| `provider` (HTTP 5xx, timeout) | Enqueue to `agent-retry` BullMQ queue, exponential backoff 2s/8s/32s. | `AGENT_ERROR` | 3 |
| `empty` (valid 200, no content) | Treated as `provider` with reason `empty`. | `AGENT_ERROR` | 3 |

Rationale for NOT retrying parse failures inline: strict mode already retries internally; a second strict call after a violation is historically as likely to fail again. The fallback guarantees the lead receives something (product requirement) and the raw output is preserved in the activity for later auditor review.

### Async-First Design

- **NestJS is async by default.** Every handler exposes `async ...(): Promise<...>`. `AgentRunnerService.processInboundMessage` is already async; preserve that.
- **The one common mistake:** awaiting Prisma writes sequentially when they could be parallelised. Handlers that write both `AgentMessage` AND `CardActivity` after a response should use `prisma.$transaction([...])` — or `Promise.all` if strict ordering isn't required. Avoid the accidental N+1 serialisation pattern used elsewhere in this codebase where each `await` is on its own line.
- **Never `await` BullMQ job results on the hot path.** The summarizer enqueues and returns; `processInboundMessage` never blocks on it. Same for the notification emit triggered by qualification — `emit` is fire-and-forget.
- **Stream vs. await:** OpenAI Responses API supports SSE streaming (`stream: true`). For Phase 5 we **await** the full response — we cannot stream *and* validate against a strict schema because partial JSON does not parse. Consider streaming only for a future "typing indicator" polish (listed in CONTEXT.md deferred ideas).
- **Event-loop trap (Node ↔ Nest):** never call `.then()` on a promise without awaiting inside a handler; a rejected promise without `await` becomes an `UnhandledPromiseRejection` warning. ESLint rule `no-floating-promises` is already enabled in the project — respect it.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

- **System vs. user separation (Responses API):**
  - `instructions` field = fully compiled persona/KB/rules, produced by `buildAgentCompiledPrompt`. Never concatenate user input into this field.
  - `input` array = only `{role: 'user'|'assistant'|'system', content}` turns. The only `system` message injected at runtime is the summary block.
- **Append the structured-output contract to the compiled prompt** (D-17). One new block at the bottom of `buildAgentCompiledPrompt`, verbatim:
  ```
  Formato de saída
  Responda SEMPRE com um objeto JSON válido no esquema definido. NÃO inclua texto antes ou depois do JSON.
  Campos:
  - should_respond (bool): true para enviar, false para segurar (fragmentação).
  - reply (string|null): texto pronto ao lead; null se should_respond=false.
  - mark_qualified (bool), qualification_reason (string|null)
  - suggested_next_stage_id (string|null): id de uma etapa válida do pipeline atual.
  - request_handoff (bool), handoff_reason (string|null)
  Se houver dúvida, prefira should_respond=false e explique brevemente em qualification_reason.
  ```
- **Few-shot: inline, not retrieved.** The conversation type is narrow (qualification chat); 2–3 exemplars embedded directly at the end of `buildAgentCompiledPrompt` is cheaper and more deterministic than dynamic RAG. No vector DB this phase.
- **Always bound `max_output_tokens`.** Never leave unset in production — unbounded generation on strict schemas can produce a valid short response OR a 4k-token repetition; cost control requires the cap. Phase 5 uses 800 default.

### Context Window Management

Conversational (not RAG, not autonomous), so the applicable pattern is **window + rolling summary**:

- **Window:** last `historyWindow` turns (default 20) loaded raw into `input` array. Covers typical 10–40 turn qualification conversations.
- **Summary:** populated by `ConversationSummarizer` every `summaryThreshold` AGENT turns (default 10). Injected as a `system` role turn at the **start** of `input` so the model sees it before any window turns. Prompt explicitly instructs "use as context, do not repeat literally".
- **Truncation fallback:** if `history.length > historyWindow`, we slice `history.slice(-historyWindow)`. No hard token counting — 20 turns × 80 tokens is ~1.6k, well below any ceiling.
- **Summary freshness race:** summarizer runs after response emit, so the **current** reply was generated against the **previous** summary. Acceptable (documented explicit trade-off) because summaries evolve slowly and the window covers recent turns.
- **Handoff/reset:** on handoff, the window is still loaded when the SDR resumes (no truncation at handoff boundary — richer context for the human). On explicit `CLOSED`, future Phase would start a fresh conversation row.

### Cost and Latency Budget

**Per-call cost (gpt-4o-mini, April 2026 pricing, confirm with OpenAI dashboard):**

| Component | Tokens | Cost (USD) |
|-----------|-------:|-----------:|
| Input: system/instructions (compiled prompt + KB) | ~4,000 | ~$0.00060 |
| Input: history window (20 × ~80) | ~1,600 | ~$0.00024 |
| Input: summary | ~500 | ~$0.000075 |
| Input: current user msg | ~50 | ~$0.0000075 |
| Output: structured reply (~800 max, typical 250) | ~250 | ~$0.00015 |
| **Total per inbound** | ~6,400 in / ~250 out | **~$0.0010** |

At 10k messages/month that is ~$10. At 100k it is ~$100. Budget fine for SMB scale; alert at 2× baseline (cost regression likely indicates prompt bloat).

**Latency target:** p50 ≤ 1.5s, p95 ≤ 4s for the structured call. Measure via request timing log in `AiService`. If p95 breaches 4s for 5 min, alert.

**Caching strategy:**

- **Exact-match cache:** not applicable — every inbound message differs and carries evolving history.
- **Semantic cache:** not valuable for conversational flow (the value is in the state, not the surface text).
- **Prompt caching (OpenAI server-side):** the compiled prompt is long and mostly stable per agent — enable OpenAI automatic prompt caching (on by default for gpt-4o-mini) by ensuring the `instructions` block is byte-identical across calls from the same agent. Concretely: do NOT embed `new Date()` or per-request variables in `instructions`. This yields ~50% discount on the cached portion after first call.

**Cheaper model routing for sub-tasks:**

| Sub-task | Model | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| Main structured decisioning | `gpt-4o-mini` | Default. Already cheapest tier with strict-output support. |
| `ConversationSummarizer` | `gpt-4o-mini` (same) | Runs async, low volume, plain text. Going cheaper (e.g., `gpt-4.1-nano` if available at the time of build) is a future optimisation — verify JSON mode NOT needed. |
| Proactive D0 greeting (existing `initiateProactiveIfAssigned`) | `gpt-4o-mini` | No structured output needed — already uses plain `generateResponse`. Keep. |

Model override per Agent lives in `Agent.profile.model` — preserves admin escape hatch to upgrade a specific agent to `gpt-4o` for higher-stakes pipelines without code changes.

### Reference Dataset

**Size:** **30 examples for v1** (exceeds 20-example production floor given the 8-dimension breadth).
- 12 critical-path / happy-path qualification conversations (proper BANT-like disclosure → `mark_qualified=true` with cited evidence).
- 6 failure-mode replicas (one per Section 1b failure mode 1-4 + 6 + 8; #5 and #7 are structural/longitudinal and are asserted by code, not examples).
- 6 adversarial / edge (multi-bubble fragmentation, price-negotiation, legal question, explicit AI challenge, explicit human handoff request, stale `suggested_next_stage_id` scenario).
- 6 bootstrapped from anonymised production traces (handpicked after v1 ships, replacing synthetic entries — **target ≥50% production-sourced by v2**).

**Storage:** Phoenix Dataset (versioned). Source file committed at `apps/api/eval/datasets/phase-05-golden-v1.jsonl` — JSONL with fields `{id, conversation_history[], user_turn, expected: {pass_rubric_ids, fail_rubric_ids}, metadata: {locale:'pt-BR', scenario_tag, anonymised_from_trace_id?}}`.

**Composition (scenario tags):**
- `HAPPY_BANT_DISCLOSURE` (×4)
- `HAPPY_MID_FUNNEL_FOLLOWUP` (×4)
- `HAPPY_HANDOFF_AT_NEGOTIATION` (×4)
- `FAIL_PREMATURE_QUALIFY` (×2)
- `FAIL_HALLUCINATED_POLICY` (×2)
- `FAIL_MISSED_HUMAN_REQUEST` (×2)
- `EDGE_FRAGMENTATION_3_BUBBLES` (×2)
- `EDGE_AI_CHALLENGE_TURN` (×2)
- `EDGE_STALE_STAGE_ID` (×1)
- `EDGE_REFUSAL_TRIGGER` (×1)
- `PROD_TRACE_ANONYMISED` (×6)

**Labeling protocol:**
1. **Senior SDR (native PT-BR)** labels rubric 1 (qualification correctness) and rubric 2 (handoff timing) per example — gold labels.
2. **Native-PT-BR WhatsApp-native reviewer** (per Section 1b role table) labels rubric 3 (tone/register) and rubric 4 (fragmentation) — gold labels.
3. **Product owner** arbitrates disagreements and owns dataset versioning (v1, v1.1, ...).
4. **Compliance reviewer (DPO)** one-time sign-off on rubric 8 (AI disclosure) wording and any tenant data included.
5. **LLM-judge calibration**: each automated judge is considered "production-ready" only when it reaches ≥0.7 Cohen's κ vs. the human gold labels on a 10-example held-out slice. Until then, it runs in **shadow mode** (logged, not gating).
6. **Prompt engineers do not label the dataset** (explicit anti-pattern avoidance per ai-evals.md pitfall #2).
7. **Bootstrap from production**: weekly, the offline flywheel surfaces 2-3 anonymised production traces for the SDR to label; approved traces feed back into the dataset. Source trace IDs are kept in `metadata.anonymised_from_trace_id` for provenance but never carry raw PII (names, phones, CPF stripped by the span processor then by a human pass).

**Anonymisation pipeline for production traces:** regex-strip (see tooling section) → manual PII pass by the reviewer → store in dataset with `metadata.locale='pt-BR'` and a synthetic lead name + phone. Raw conversation IDs are kept in a separate LGPD-access-controlled table, never in the dataset JSONL.

## 6. Guardrails

### Online (Real-Time)

Each guardrail is a hard check inside the handler cascade (`apps/api/src/agent/handlers/*`). Kept minimal — every guardrail adds latency.

| # | Guardrail | Trigger | Check Location | Intervention |
|---|-----------|---------|----------------|--------------|
| G1 | **Zod schema-compliance enforcement** | `AiService.generateStructuredResponse` returns `{ok:false, reason:'parse'}` OR Zod `safeParse` fails in `StructuredReplyGenerator`. | `StructuredReplyGenerator` (immediately post-call). | **Fallback** per D-18: raw text becomes `reply`, `should_respond=true`, other fields defaulted false/null. Persist `CardActivity AGENT_PARSE_FALLBACK` with `metadata.raw_output`. Lead always receives *something*. |
| G2 | **Refusal handling** | `AiService.generateStructuredResponse` returns `{ok:false, reason:'refusal'}` (OpenAI `output[].content[].type === 'refusal'`). | `StructuredReplyGenerator`. | **Hold** — coerce to `{should_respond:false}`, write `CardActivity AGENT_HELD` with `metadata.reason='model_refusal'` and `metadata.raw_refusal`. Emit `AGENT_QUALIFIED_READY_TO_ADVANCE`-style notification but with `NotificationType.AGENT_REFUSAL_REVIEW` to the owner SDR (reuse notification module, one new type). No outbound WhatsApp send. |
| G3 | **Stale / cross-pipeline `suggested_next_stage_id` validation** | `reply.suggested_next_stage_id !== null` AND `prisma.stage.findFirst({where:{id, pipelineId: card.pipelineId, deletedAt: null}})` returns null. | `QualificationHandler` (right before writing `AGENT_QUALIFIED` activity). | **Flag** — write the activity with `metadata.suggested_next_stage_id=null` and `metadata.invalid_suggested_stage_id=<offending id>`. Kanban CTA then falls back to "Agente marcou qualificado — selecione destino" per D-25. Increment `stage_id_invalid_total` metric. |
| G4 | **AI-disclosure honesty gate** (WhatsApp 2026 policy + Dim 8) | Inbound turn matches regex `/\b(é um (robô\|bot\|robo)\|é (uma )?(ia\|ai)\|você é humano\|é automático\|isso é automatizado\|fala com (pessoa\|humano)\b/i` AND agent output does NOT contain any affirmation keyword (`sou.*(ia\|assistente\|agente\|virtual\|automatizado)`). | Post-generation, before `OutboundDispatcher.send`. | **Block + rewrite.** Replace `reply` with the operator-configured disclosure template (default: "Oi! Sou um agente virtual do time da {tenant}, respondendo aqui no WhatsApp. Posso continuar te ajudando ou prefere falar com um atendente humano?") and coerce `request_handoff=true` if the lead's phrasing was assertive. Log `CardActivity AGENT_DISCLOSURE_ENFORCED`. |
| G5 | **Consecutive-agent-reply throttle** (Dim 4 fragmentation safety-net) | Within the current conversation, last AGENT `AgentMessage.createdAt` is <10s ago AND there is no intervening USER message. | `OutboundDispatcher` immediately before `whatsappService.sendMessage`. | **Hold** — coerce current turn to `should_respond=false`, write `CardActivity AGENT_HELD` with `metadata.reason='throttle_consecutive'`. Prevents the prompt-driven discipline from catastrophically failing against WhatsApp spam heuristics. |
| G6 | **Output moderation (lightweight, opt-in per tenant)** | Agent reply contains terms from a tenant-configurable blocklist (default includes profanity + brand-forbidden terms set in `Agent.profile.blockedTerms`). | `OutboundDispatcher`. | **Escalate** — coerce `request_handoff=true`, replace reply with "Vou transferir para um atendente humano." Log `CardActivity AGENT_HANDOFF` with `metadata.reason='moderation_block'`. |
| G7 | **Commercial-commitment fabrication guard** (CDC risk, Failure #2) | Agent reply contains currency patterns (`/R\$\s*\d/`, `%\s*(desconto\|off)/i`, `/sla/i`, `/prazo de (entrega\|implantação\|pagamento)/i`, `/emit(imos\|e) (nf\|nota)/i`) AND the configured KB corpus does NOT contain any matching substring. | `OutboundDispatcher`. | **Escalate** — coerce `request_handoff=true`, rewrite reply to a deflection template ("Deixa eu confirmar esse detalhe com um atendente humano."). Log `CardActivity AGENT_COMMERCIAL_DEFLECTION` with `metadata.matched_pattern` and `metadata.reply_redacted`. |
| G8 | **LGPD opt-out recognition** | Inbound turn matches `/\b(cancelar|descadastrar|parar de receber|não quero mais|unsubscribe|remover meus dados)\b/i`. | Pre-handler, inside `AgentRunnerService.processInboundMessage`. | **Hard-stop** — do NOT invoke model. Write `CardActivity LEAD_OPT_OUT` with the matched phrase. Update `AgentConversation.status='CLOSED'`. Notify owner via `NotificationType.LEAD_OPT_OUT`. Send a one-time confirmation template message (operator-configurable; default: "Entendido. Não enviaremos mais mensagens por aqui. Se mudar de ideia, é só responder este número."). |

### Human-in-the-loop Checkpoint

**Qualification-advance checkpoint (D-01 hybrid mode, reaffirmed):** `mark_qualified=true` NEVER moves the card. Stage advance requires the SDR to click "Mover para [Stage Name] (sugerido pelo agente)" (D-25) OR to move the card manually. This is the load-bearing human-in-the-loop: the SDR's subsequent `MOVE` (or lack thereof) is the ground-truth signal for Dimension 1. The UI must not auto-confirm after timeout — stale qualified badges are an intentional visible-debt signal.

### Offline (Flywheel)

Runs on a BullMQ queue `agent-eval-flywheel` with a nightly cron (03:00 BRT). Pulls the previous 24h of closed conversations from `AgentConversation` WHERE `status IN ('CLOSED','HANDOFF_REQUIRED')` OR last AGENT turn >24h ago.

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Qualification precision (Dim 1)** — `mark_qualified=true` AND SDR confirmed `MOVE` within 48h ÷ total `mark_qualified=true` | 100% of qualified conversations in the 24h window. Stratified by agent (per-tenant dashboard). | Weekly review by Sales Ops. If precision <0.70 for any agent 2 consecutive weeks → product owner opens a prompt-revision ticket + holds that agent's qualification badge behind a feature flag. |
| **Handoff necessity (Dim 2)** — of handoffs, % that the SDR actually completed a handoff-worthy action vs. bounced back to agent | 100% of handoffs. | If SDRs mark >30% of handoffs as unnecessary for 2 weeks, tune the prompt's escalation guidance. |
| **Fragmentation cascade rate (Dim 4)** — conversations with ≥1 burst of >1 AGENT reply within 10s | 100% of conversations. | Alert to product owner at >2% cascade rate per agent. Likely cause: operator removed the fragmentation-handling block from the prompt. |
| **Parse-fallback rate (Dim 5)** — `AGENT_PARSE_FALLBACK` activities ÷ total AGENT turns | 100%. | >1% daily triggers an investigation of the compiled prompt (often caused by an operator inserting conflicting instructions that overwhelm the JSON contract). |
| **Stage-id invalidation rate (G3)** — invalid `suggested_next_stage_id` ÷ total `mark_qualified=true` | 100%. | >5% triggers investigation of pipeline-change cadence (likely the operator is editing stages mid-conversation). |
| **Cost regression** — mean input-tokens per turn, week-over-week | 100%. | ≥2× baseline = prompt-bloat alert. |
| **Tone/register sample (Dim 3)** | Stratified random sample, 20 conversations/week per active agent. LLM-judge scored, Human spot-check on 5/week. | Per-agent aggregated score <4.0 = prompt coach session with operator. |
| **Memory drift (Dim 7)** | 10 conversations/week where `messageCount >= summaryThreshold*2`. Compare summary against source window. | Drop-rate of "citable facts" >30% triggers summarizer-prompt revision. |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** **Arize Phoenix (self-hosted)** — see Section 5 setup. Hooks into the existing BullMQ queue observability and the `CardActivity` audit trail (NOT a parallel pipeline per `<input>` constraint).

**Instrumentation contract:**
- Every `AgentRunnerService.processInboundMessage` invocation is one root span (`agent.processInboundMessage`) with attributes: `conversation.id`, `card.id`, `agent.id`, `tenant.id`, `model`, `history.size`, `summary.present`, `fallback.used`.
- Each handler is a child span (`agent.history.load`, `agent.structured.generate`, `agent.qualification.check`, `agent.handoff.check`, `agent.outbound.dispatch`, `agent.summarize`).
- The OpenAI call is auto-instrumented by `@arizeai/openinference-instrumentation-openai` — captures `prompt`, `response`, `token counts`, `latency`. PII stripped by the span processor (see anonymisation pipeline).
- Every `CardActivity` write emits a span event `cardActivity.write` with `activity.type` — lets Phoenix correlate runtime traces with audit trail.
- BullMQ `agent-retry` and `agent-summarize` jobs each create a span linked to the originating request via traceparent injection.

**Alert Thresholds (paging):**

| Severity | Condition | Channel |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **P0 (page on-call)** | `schema.parse.fail.rate` >5% over 15 min OR `latency.p95` >10s over 10 min OR any `NotificationType.AGENT_PERSISTENT_FAILURE` fires OR G4 disclosure gate triggered >0 in last hour AND agent reply was a denial. | PagerDuty + Slack `#saaso-oncall` |
| **P1 (Slack, business hours)** | `parse.fail.rate` >2% hourly OR `refusal.rate` >1% daily OR `qualified.to_confirmed.ratio` <0.50 for 48h OR `stage_id.invalid.rate` >10% daily. | Slack `#saaso-ai-health` |
| **P2 (weekly report)** | Dimension 3/4/7 scores trending down week-over-week OR cost regression >2× baseline OR fragmentation cascade >2% weekly. | Weekly report email to product owner + sales ops lead. |

**Smart Sampling Strategy:**

Not every conversation gets human review — signal-based routing:

1. **Always-sample (100%)**: any conversation with `AGENT_PARSE_FALLBACK`, `AGENT_DISCLOSURE_ENFORCED`, `AGENT_COMMERCIAL_DEFLECTION`, `AGENT_REFUSAL_REVIEW`, or `AGENT_ERROR` activity. Routed to the `#saaso-ai-health` triage queue with trace link.
2. **Qualified-but-not-advanced (48h timeout)**: any conversation with `AGENT_QUALIFIED` where no subsequent `MOVE` within 48h. High-signal candidate for Dimension 1 calibration — goes to the SDR-labelling queue.
3. **Qualified-and-reverted**: any conversation where SDR moved the card back within 24h of an `AGENT_QUALIFIED`. Strong negative signal — auto-added as a "candidate failure" for the reference dataset.
4. **Handoff-bounced-back**: handoffs where SDR reopened the agent (`toggleTakeover` → agent resume) within 10 min — indicates over-escalation (Dim 2 score=3).
5. **Stratified random**: 5 per agent per week regardless of flags — guards against blind spots in the signal-based routing itself.
6. **Adversarial-challenge turns (G4 matches)**: 100% sampled, irrespective of outcome, stored in a compliance review bucket retained for 24 months (LGPD + CDC evidence window).

**Drift Detection Strategy:**

Phoenix experiments run weekly on the 7-day rolling production sample against the v-current golden dataset:
- **Embedding-based semantic drift** (reply content): compute mean cosine distance between the last 7-day sample and the dataset embeddings (via `text-embedding-3-small`, one-off batch). Alert on >20% shift.
- **Rubric-score drift**: LLM-judge re-runs Dimensions 2, 3, 4, 8 on a 100-conversation weekly sample. Alert when any dimension's mean score drops >0.3 vs. the prior 4-week rolling mean.
- **Input-distribution drift**: track mean/std of `inbound.length`, `history.size`, `summary.length` per agent. Alert on ≥2σ deviation sustained 3 days — usually caused by a new channel going live or a prompt edit.
- **Provider drift**: OpenAI model updates are silent. Weekly smoke run: execute all 30 golden examples against current production model, compare field-level outputs to the locked expected-behaviour set. Any change in binary fields (`should_respond`, `mark_qualified`, `request_handoff`) on >10% of examples = drift alert + model pin review.
